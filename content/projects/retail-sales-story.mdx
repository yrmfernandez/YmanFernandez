## Context

A retailer saw quarterly revenue fall 14% and didn't know why. The leadership
question wasn't "build a model" — it was "explain this, clearly, to people who
don't read code." This project is about **communication as much as analysis**.

I worked from a 2.3M-row transaction table spanning two years across categories,
regions, and price points.

## Approach

I treated the dip as a decomposition problem: revenue is price × units, across
products and regions, so I broke the change down along each axis to find where it
concentrated.

- Pulled and shaped the data in SQL, keeping the transformations reproducible.
- Decomposed the year-over-year change by category, region, and SKU to isolate
  the contributors rather than eyeballing totals.
- Built a small set of **annotated** Plotly charts — each one makes a single
  point, with the takeaway written on the chart, not left for the reader to infer.

## Result

Two SKUs and a mid-quarter price increase accounted for the majority of the
decline; most of the catalog was flat or up. The write-up gave leadership a
specific, actionable story instead of a scary aggregate number — and pointed to a
pricing decision they could revisit.

## Reflection

I'd pair this with a lightweight dashboard so the decomposition updates itself
each quarter, turning a one-off investigation into an ongoing monitor. I'd also
add a simple significance check to separate real shifts from noise in the
smaller regional segments.
