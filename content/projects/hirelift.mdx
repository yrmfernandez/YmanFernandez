## Context

Most resumes are read first by an Applicant Tracking System, not a person — and a
resume that a human likes can still be filtered out before anyone sees it.
HireLift is an AI resume builder that generates **ATS-friendly** resumes: upload
an existing resume or fill out a form, paste a job description, and it produces an
optimized resume plus preparation guidance and role recommendations.

## Approach

The core is a **multi-agent pipeline**, each agent with one job:

- **Extractor** — parses the job description and the user's details into
  structured data.
- **Writer** — drafts an ATS-friendly resume from that structure.
- **Judge** — evaluates the draft and loops feedback to the writer until it
  passes.
- **Coach** — builds a tailored preparation plan for the target role.
- **Roles** — recommends job titles with fit ratings.
- plus **helper agents** for skill suggestions and parsing uploaded files.

The Writer–Judge feedback loop is the interesting part: instead of trusting a
single generation, the draft has to clear an automated reviewer before it ships.
It's built on **Node.js + Express** with the **Groq API** (running models like
llama-3.3-70b), a no-build vanilla frontend, and Vercel serverless functions.

## Result

The app produces tailored, ATS-oriented resumes with PDF/DOCX export, saves
progress in localStorage, offers a light/dark theme, and includes a "mock mode"
for testing without spending API calls. Deploying it also meant working within
real platform limits — tuning serverless function timeouts for pipeline runs that
can exceed 60 seconds, and a 4 MB upload cap to stay under Vercel's constraints.

## Reflection

The Judge currently approves on general quality; a stronger version would score
the draft against the *specific* job description's keywords and requirements, so
"ATS-friendly" becomes measurable rather than aspirational. Caching the extractor
output would also cut both latency and cost on repeat runs.
