## Context

Choice paralysis is a small, everyday tax on attention. QuickDecide is a
"zero-friction" AI decision engine: you hand it a set of options and it returns a
definitive recommendation — a winner, a score, and concise reasoning — without
asking you to log in, and without storing a database or chat history.

The design constraint was statelessness: everything happens in a single request,
which keeps it fast, private, and cheap to run.

## Approach

Rather than one big prompt, I built an **agentic pipeline** of three specialized
LLM agents:

- a **classifier** that reads the decision and figures out what kind it is,
- a **judge** that scores the options, and
- a **responder** that crafts the final recommendation and reasoning.

Around that core I added the touches that make it feel considered: three modes
(Serious, Funny, Instant), a "wildcard" that proposes an unconventional option
when the given ones are weak, and two contextual sliders that change depending on
the decision type. Small tools give the model real-world grounding — weather
lookup, Manila-timezone and date awareness, and cost comparison.

Technically it's **Next.js 15 (App Router) + React 19** with **Groq** via the
Vercel AI SDK, **Zod** for validation, and **Server Actions** so API keys never
reach the browser.

## Result

The app ships a clean "receipt" for every decision — winner, tie status, scoring,
and reasoning — and is deployed on Vercel. It's a compact demonstration of a
multi-agent LLM pattern that stays stateless and keeps secrets server-side.

## Reflection

The obvious next step is evaluation: right now quality is judged by feel, and I'd
add a small labeled set of decisions to measure whether the agents actually agree
with human preferences. I'd also expose *why* the judge scored an option the way
it did, so the reasoning is auditable rather than just persuasive.
